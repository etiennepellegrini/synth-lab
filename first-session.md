# Your First Session with the Volca Keys

A guided 45–60 minute hands-on tutorial. Work through these exercises in order — each one builds on the last. Plug in headphones or connect to a speaker for the best experience.

---

## 0. Setup (5 min)

1. **Power**: Insert 6 AA batteries or plug in your 9V DC adapter (center-positive, EIAJ-03 barrel).
2. **Audio**: Plug headphones or a 3.5mm TRS cable into the headphone jack. The internal speaker cuts out when you do this.
3. **Power on**: Flip the switch. You'll see LEDs flash during self-tuning (takes a few seconds).
4. **Volume**: Turn the **VOLUME** knob to about 50%. You can adjust later.
5. **Starting clean**: Hold **FUNC + step 16** to clear the current pattern so you're working with a blank slate.

> **Tip**: The Volca auto-powers-off after 4 hours of inactivity. You can disable this in the global settings (hold FUNC at power-on, then press keyboard button 1).

---

## 1. First Sound — Just Play (5 min)

Touch the **ribbon keyboard** and listen. You should hear a sound immediately.

**Try these things:**
- Slide your finger across the ribbon. Notice how pitch changes smoothly (portamento).
- Tap different "keys" — the ribbon spans F to G (27 notes).
- Turn the **OCTAVE** knob to shift between registers (32' is very low, 1' is very high). Find a range you like.

**What's happening:** All three oscillators are sounding in whatever voice mode is currently selected. The ribbon is a touch-sensitive surface, not mechanical keys, so there's no click or resistance — just touch.

---

## 2. Voice Mode Tour (5 min)

Now slowly turn the **VOICE** knob through all 6 positions. Play the same note (or notes) in each mode and listen carefully:

| Position | What You'll Hear | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Poly** | Play 2-3 fingers: you hear a chord | Each oscillator plays a different note (sawtooth waves) |
| **Unison** | Thick, fat single note | All 3 oscillators play the same pitch |
| **Octave** | Full, spread-out tone | One oscillator is pitched an octave higher |
| **Fifth** | Naturally harmonious, organ-like | One oscillator is a musical fifth above |
| **Unison Ring** | Metallic, harsh, robotic | Square waves through ring modulation |
| **Poly Ring** | Dissonant chords, atonal | Ring-modulated square waves, individually pitched |

**Exercise**: Set the voice to **Unison**. Now slowly turn the **DETUNE** knob. At center (12 o'clock) all three oscillators are in tune. Sweep it and hear them spread apart — this detuned unison is one of the fattest sounds in synthesis.

> **Bookmark this feel**: Unison + slight detune is your go-to "big lead" sound.

---

## 3. Filter Sculpting (10 min)

This is where sound design really begins. Set the voice to **Unison** (for a clear, harmonically rich tone to filter).

### Exercise 3a: The Cutoff Sweep
- Set **PEAK** (resonance) to zero (fully counter-clockwise).
- Hold a note on the ribbon.
- Slowly sweep the **CUTOFF** knob from fully open (clockwise) down to fully closed.
- Listen: bright → muffled → almost silent. You're removing harmonics from the top down.

### Exercise 3b: Adding Resonance
- Set cutoff to about 12 o'clock (middle).
- Slowly increase **PEAK** from zero to maximum while holding a note.
- Listen for the resonant "singing" quality that develops — a sharp emphasis right at the cutoff frequency.
- At maximum Peak, you may hear the filter **self-oscillate** — producing its own whistle/sine tone.

### Exercise 3c: Resonant Sweep
- Set Peak to about 60-70%.
- Now sweep Cutoff slowly while holding a note.
- This is the classic "filter sweep" sound — you'll recognize it instantly from electronic music.

> **This is your most powerful tool.** The filter cutoff knob is the parameter you'll reach for most often during performance.

---

## 4. Envelope Shaping (10 min)

### Exercise 4a: Attack
- Play a note and adjust the **ATTACK** knob.
- At minimum (CCW): the sound starts instantly — percussive, like a pluck.
- At maximum (CW): the sound fades in slowly — like a pad or a bowed string.
- Try a slow attack with the Poly voice mode for a lush evolving chord.

### Exercise 4b: Decay/Release
- The **DECAY/RELEASE** knob controls both the decay (how fast the sound drops from peak to sustain level after attack) AND the release (how long the sound lingers after you lift your finger). They are linked to a single knob.
- At minimum: the sound cuts off sharply.
- At maximum: long decay and long fade-out after release.
- Try: slow attack + long decay/release + Poly mode = instant ambient pad.

### Exercise 4c: Sustain
- The **SUSTAIN** knob sets the level the sound holds while your finger is on the ribbon.
- At maximum: the sound stays at full volume while held.
- At minimum: the sound decays to silence even while you're holding the note.
- Combined with a moderate Decay/Release, lower sustain values create pluck-like sounds.

### Exercise 4d: Pitch Envelope (VCO EG INT)
- Set attack to minimum (instant) and sustain to a moderate level.
- Find the **EG INT** knob in the **VCO section** (this controls envelope → pitch).
- Turn it clockwise from center and play a note — hear the pitch bend effect.
- Turn it counter-clockwise from center — the pitch bends in the opposite direction.
- Small amounts = subtle pitch character. Large amounts = dramatic laser/zap effects.

### Exercise 4e: Filter Envelope (CUTOFF EG INT)
- Now find the **EG INT** knob in the **VCF section** (labeled "CUTOFF EG INT" — this controls envelope → filter cutoff).
- Set your filter cutoff fairly low (around 9-10 o'clock).
- Turn the CUTOFF EG INT clockwise and play notes — the filter opens briefly at the start of each note, creating a plucky, percussive attack.
- This is one of the most important routings in synthesis — it creates the classic "envelope filter" sound.

---

## 5. LFO Experiments (5 min)

### Exercise 5a: Vibrato (Pitch Modulation)
- Turn the **PITCH INT** knob (in the LFO section) to about 20-30%.
- Set **RATE** to a moderate speed (10-11 o'clock).
- Play a note. You should hear vibrato — just like the vibrato you'd do on flute!
- The LFO waveform changes the character:
  - **Triangle**: smooth, natural vibrato
  - **Square**: jarring pitch trill
  - **Saw**: rising or falling pitch ramp

### Exercise 5b: Filter Wah (Cutoff Modulation)
- Reset Pitch INT to zero.
- Turn the **CUTOFF INT** knob (cutoff modulation, also in the LFO section) up to ~40%.
- Set the filter cutoff to around 10 o'clock (somewhat closed).
- Play and hold a note: wah-wah-wah-wah. The LFO is sweeping the filter open and shut.
- Adjust Rate: slow = gentle sweep, fast = aggressive wobble.

> **Note:** The Volca Keys' LFO can modulate pitch and filter cutoff, but not amplitude. There's no tremolo on this synth.

---

## 6. Delay Exploration (5 min)

- Turn **DELAY TIME** to about 12 o'clock.
- Turn **DELAY FEEDBACK** to about 30-40%.
- Play some notes and listen to the echoes.

**Try these:**
- **Short time, moderate feedback**: slapback echo, adds thickness.
- **Long time, high feedback**: spacey, ambient repeats that build up.
- **Move the TIME knob while echoes are sounding**: the pitch warps, like a tape echo machine. This is one of the most fun things on the Volca Keys.
- **Maximum feedback**: the echoes never die — they build up and self-oscillate. Use carefully!
- **TEMPO SYNC on** (FUNC + step 14): the echoes lock to the sequencer tempo.

> **Warning**: High feedback can get very loud very fast. Keep your volume moderate while experimenting.

---

## 7. Your First Sequence (10 min)

### Recording a Loop
1. Set a tempo with the **TEMPO** knob (try 100-120 BPM).
2. Press **PLAY** — the step LEDs start chasing. You won't hear anything yet.
3. Press **REC** (the record indicator lights up).
4. Play notes on the ribbon keyboard. Each note you play is captured on the current step.
5. The pattern is 16 steps long. Let it loop a few times, adding notes via overdub.
6. Press **REC** again to stop recording. Your loop keeps playing.

### Editing
- Made a mistake? Press **REC**, then hold **FUNC** and tap the step you want to erase during playback.
- Want to start over? Hold **FUNC + step 16** to clear the pattern.

### Changing Pattern Speed
- **FUNC + step 1**: normal speed
- **FUNC + step 2**: half speed (each step lasts twice as long)
- **FUNC + step 3**: quarter speed (very slow)

---

## 8. Motion Recording (5 min)

This is where the magic happens.

1. Start a pattern playing (one you recorded in step 7, or a factory pattern).
2. Press **REC** to enter record mode.
3. **While the sequence plays**, grab the **CUTOFF** knob and slowly sweep it.
4. Press **REC** to stop recording.
5. Listen: the filter sweep is now part of the pattern! The cutoff knob's LED will flash to show it has motion data.

**Try recording motion on other knobs too:**
- LFO Rate (creates evolving modulation)
- Delay Time (pitch-warping echoes in the pattern)
- Detune (oscillators spreading and converging)
- Attack (changing articulation across the pattern)
- CUTOFF EG INT (changing how "plucky" each note sounds across the loop)

> **Note**: Voice mode and Peak (resonance) are the only knobs that can NOT be motion-recorded.

---

## 9. Putting It All Together (5 min)

Build a complete sound + pattern from scratch:

1. **Choose a voice**: Unison for a lead, Poly for chords.
2. **Shape the tone**: Set cutoff to taste, add a touch of resonance.
3. **Add movement**: A little LFO on the filter, or slow attack for pads.
4. **Record a sequence**: 16 steps, keep it simple at first.
5. **Add motion**: Record a filter sweep or LFO rate change over the loop.
6. **Add delay**: Dial in some echo for space and atmosphere.
7. **Save it**: Hold **FUNC**, press **MEMORY**, then press a **step button (1-8)** to save to that slot.

Congratulations — you've just designed a sound, programmed a sequence, automated parameters, and saved a pattern. That's real synthesis.

---

## What's Next?

- **Active Step tricks**: During playback, press FUNC + PLAY to enter Active Step mode. Toggle steps on/off to shorten the pattern or create irregular rhythms.
- **Flux mode**: FUNC + step 15 to disable step quantization — notes land wherever you play them, creating a looser, more human groove.
- **MIDI control**: Connect a MIDI keyboard to the 5-pin DIN input for a real keyboard experience. The Volca Keys responds to velocity and pitch bend over MIDI.
- **Sync with other gear**: Use a 3.5mm mono cable from Sync Out to another Volca's Sync In to lock tempos.
- **Explore the factory patterns**: Switch between patterns using FUNC + step keys 1-8 during playback.
- **LFO trigger sync**: FUNC + step 11 toggles whether the LFO restarts with each note (consistent vibrato) or runs freely (organic variation).
